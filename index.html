Precision Muon Physics Group

Welcome to the

Precision Muon
Physics Group

at the University of Washington

The Precision Muon Physics Group joined CENPA in 2010. We built the effort at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where Dave Hertzog was a faculty member since 1986 and Peter Kammel since 2000. Our mission is to identify compelling, precision experiments that either determine fundamental quantities in physics or sensitively test the Standard Model. Students and postdocs in our group take on leading roles in hardware design and development, all operational aspects of running a complex beam experiment, Monte Carlo simulation, data analysis, and presentation. As a rule, we work collectively on all experiments, but each group member has a specific main focus.

Current Experiments

Muon g-2

A measurement of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, one of the most promising signals of physics beyond the standard model, to 140 ppb

MuSun

MuSun will be the first precision measurement of this constant in the 2N sector and will help to calibrate the basic astrophysics reactions.
